What changes

When your coach knows exactly where the dart landed

Manual entry tells DartsHQ your score. Autodarts tells it your coordinates. That's the difference between "you hit 60" and "your last three darts drifted 8mm left — aim right of the treble wire."

Grouping drift analysis

DartsHQ plots where your darts actually land versus where you're aiming — and tells you whether you're drifting left, right, high, or low, and by how much.

Session heatmaps

Every throw logged as a coordinate, visualised as a heatmap on the board. See patterns across a session or across months of play.

Aim-point optimisation

Based on your variance, DartsHQ calculates whether T20 or T19 is genuinely the better target for your current level — and shows you the maths.

Between-dart corrections

Because the coach knows exactly where each dart landed, the Audio Coach can give corrections between darts rather than after the visit — while there's still time to adjust.

How it works under the hood

Built respectfully,
on top of what already works

The DartsHQ Connector reads from your local Autodarts MQTT broker — the same data bus your Autodarts client uses internally. It never touches your Autodarts account, never interacts with Autodarts servers, and only reads data that your own hardware is already generating on your own network. Your credentials stay entirely with Autodarts.

The connector is fully open-source. Every line of code is publicly available on GitHub for anyone who wants to verify exactly what it does. This is how third-party darts tools in this community have always worked — reading local data to build better experiences on top.
